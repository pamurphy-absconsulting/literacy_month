To begin: context, as we've covered in depth, is the information that we're providing to the model. There are a lot of things that fit under the definition of context. This could be all information within our conversation — the entire history. It could be the specific prompts or instructions that we're writing. This could be information that's been retrieved out of different files that we've uploaded, and it could also be sets of information retrieved from internal knowledge systems like HR. There are a lot of different components of what we call context.

When I talk about workspace context, what I'm really referring to is the idea of shared background information that's stored and set — always made available to the model within a specific chat or series of chats. What a workspace ultimately aims to address is allowing multiple team members to share context, to share information, to share memories and files, in such a way where we're all working within chats that are pulling from the same key pieces of information. All sorts of different information is loaded in the background and made available to the model as part of its context to further ground and guide its responses.

So within Sierra, you would click into a workspace and start setting certain key pieces of context — certain files that you want accessible across all threads. Then you can invite members into the workspace so that you can collaborate further. Not necessarily within one chat, but everything done within that workspace is now brought into this more persistent contextual space, such that your team members are pulling in a lot of the same information.

Now, the thread content you're working with remains private — the information you store within a thread stays with you. But if you introduce a memory, it's now stored within the context of the workspace. If you upload a file to the workspace, it's now accessible to every other member of the workspace in their own chats.

The real value of workspaces is that we can configure what are called task modes. These are essentially reusable prompts specific to that workspace. If we can collaborate as a team and really dial in certain prompts, we can save them — and other members of the workspace can then access that same prompt or set of instructions more seamlessly.
