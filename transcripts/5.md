# Day 5 — Context

Hi, welcome back everyone. This is Sierra Literacy Month. We'll continue to break down general concepts as it relates to artificial intelligence. And then as we progress, we will be focusing more and more on specific applications within Sierra and the best ways to really leverage this within your workflow and your day-to-day at ABS.

Now up until now, I've focused more on certain foundational concepts just to give us a better grasp of artificial intelligence and large language models — generally what we're working with and how to interact with it. The last one was focused more on the kind of information that these models have been exposed to, that you could almost think of as their internal knowledge base — the kind of information that they "know." And in this one, I want to focus more on context, which again is the kind of information that we provide. And this is ultimately the only thing we really have control over.

As discussed, these models are fixed. They're exposed to massive amounts of training data and up until that point, they're not shifting, they're not dynamically changing, learning, evolving based on the interactions that we have. It's better to think of the existing model in its current static state as that of an engine — a reasoning engine, to be more precise. It's built out highly sophisticated statistical relations to understand how words relate to one another. It's understood generally how to reason. And the raw materials that we provide is what it's going to reason over, to allow it to really generate the sort of responses and answers and do some more of the work that we hope to accomplish within the ABS context.

Right now, specifically on what context includes — this could be a number of different things. Obviously when I first open up Sierra and I write hello, whatever I type into the chat window there, that hello — that would be considered context. Now I do think it's important to recognize as I move through the conversation, the model itself is not learning based on the chat history up to that point. What's actually happening is that every time I type something in and I press enter, it's simply taking the entirety of the conversation history up to that point and reprocessing it. So the entire conversation history should be considered as context in this specific application — not just what I type into the window with my last message. So it's all the conversation history up to that point, obviously whatever I typed into the window within that specific run, and then any information that I may have uploaded via attachments or files. It'll be pulling in from those and of course injecting that and then reasoning over that. We'll of course talk more in depth about file uploads and really how it's working with attachments and pulling information out of that.

But for now, just to recognize that when we think of our context window — the information that we provide — it's primarily going to be the entire conversation history, whatever specific question or set of instructions I provide for it within the window there, and then information pulled from the files that I've uploaded.

Now, I do want to reiterate once more that the model itself is static. It's not shifting, it's not changing. It knows what it knows. What we really control is the kind of information that we provide. And this is so fundamentally important because at the end of the day, the model simply can't know what it doesn't know. As it relates to more specific ABS content — contract data, project information, even employee information — it's not going to necessarily have this information based on its training data. So it's really up to us to provide it with all necessary information, all necessary context, that it can reliably reason over to give us the answer that we're looking for.

So in the next, we'll start talking more about some of the strategies that we do here at the CoE to really bolster the kinds of information and internal knowledge systems that the models can then pull from — so that it's not necessarily starting with zero in the way of context — building out systems to go ahead and bolster it with more of the information that we would like for it to have that the model itself doesn't already have based on its training data.

So as always, really appreciate your time and attention. Until next time.
