## What are Large Language Models (LLMs)?

[Diagram: A dark background with a flow diagram. On the left, a blue arrow labeled "Text Input" points to a central circle labeled "Large Language Model." On the right, an orange arrow labeled "Text Output" points outward. Below the diagram is a rectangular box with the heading "Core Ideas" and three bullet points.]

### Core Ideas
- An LLM is a calculator that converts a text input (a prompt) to a text output (a completion).
- It stores a large quantity of digital information that it uses in its calculations, giving the appearance of knowledge and logic: Artificial Intelligence.
  - The calculator injects randomness to create human-like responses.


## What is LLM Training?

![Diagram: Illustration of LLM training process with a stack of servers and three circles labeled 2024, 2025, and 2026, connected by an arrow.]

### Core Ideas
- Training is using large amounts of information to calibrate a generative model so that it imitates human intelligence.
- LLMs are trained by major AI companies: OpenAI, Anthropic, xAI, Google.
- LLMs do not automatically learn from being used.
- **ABS does NOT train its own LLMs.**

---

## What else can LLMs "know"?

![Diagram: Two overlapping circles labeled "Context" (blue) and "Internal LLM Knowledge" (yellow), with a plus sign between them.]

### Core Ideas
- The LLM has its own general knowledge that "comes with it."
- It can also react to external knowledge, "Context," that is provided to it.
- **There is no other knowledge available to it.**


## What kinds of knowledge are "internal" to an LLM?

### Diagram Description:
- A Venn diagram with three overlapping circles in the center:
  - **Public Knowledge**
  - **Historical Events**
  - **General Logic**
- Surrounding the Venn diagram are three additional labeled circles:
  - **Corporate IP** (on the left)
  - **Recent Events** (below)
  - **Deep Domain Knowledge** (on the right)
- The Venn diagram is enclosed by a dashed yellow circle, with arrows pointing outward to the surrounding circles.

---

### Core Ideas:
- Therefore, they know:
  - Publicly available knowledge.
  - Events that occurred prior to the creation of the training data.
  - Logic that is inferred from these sources.
- Their internal knowledge does not include:
  - Corporate IP.
  - Recent History.
  - Deep Technical Knowledge.
- AI providers are constantly expanding the limits of internal knowledge.

## What is a Hallucination?

### Diagram Description:
- A central emoji-like face with star-shaped eyes and a smile.
- A blue arrow pointing left and a red arrow pointing right:
  - The blue arrow represents something neutral or positive.
  - The red arrow with an exclamation mark represents something incorrect or alarming.

---

### Core Ideas:
- A "hallucination" is "high probability," but inaccurate, output from an LLM.
- The individual words and phrases make sense, but the answer is wrong.
- This often occurs around the "edges" of the LLM's training data/knowledge.

---

## What is fine-tuning?

![Illustration of a Pac-Man-like figure consuming circles, symbolizing the concept of fine-tuning.]

### Core Ideas
- Fine-tuning is doing additional training on an already-trained LLM.
  - It usually diminishes the general accuracy of the LLM.
- It can improve an LLM’s knowledge of specific information or styles.
  - It is a way to convert context into “internal knowledge.”
- Think of it like brainwashing… the brainwashed LLM may learn to respond properly to certain questions, but becomes unstable in other areas.
- By providing better context, you can generally produce the safe effects as fine-tuning, without the negative side-effects.
