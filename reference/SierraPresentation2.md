## About Tokens:
- **LLMs technically do not process text directly—they process tokens.**
- Tokens are pieces of semantic meaning that can be represented numerically.
  - **LL** → [1.2323, 0.223, -4.797, 3.156, …]
  - **Ms** → [-0.156, 3.583, 0.213, -9.397, …]
  - **technically** → [-4.897, 2.196, 1.272, …]

## Core Ideas
- A "hallucination" is "high probability," but inaccurate, output from an LLM.
- The individual words and phrases make sense, but the answer is wrong.

## Components of a Good Prompt
- **Context**: What background information does the LLM need?
- **Task**: What specifically do you want it to do?
- **Format**: Specify the output structure (number of words, bullets, narrative, table, etc.).
- **Tone**: How should it sound?
- **Examples/Constraints**: What do you need for success?

## Basic Prompt Tasks
- **Search**: "What is ____?"
- **Generation**: "Write an email saying ____."
- **Transformation**: "Take this ____ and make it more ____."
- **Analysis**: "Analyze ____ to help me understand ____."
- **Role-play**: "Talk to me as if you were a ____ to help me ____."

## Core Idea
- Don't overthink prompting. Pretend like you are sending requests to a hyperactive intern who will attempt anything that you ask. Sometimes it won’t work, but many times it will.

---

#### Speech to Text
- **IF:** you don’t know exactly what you want OR you have a lot of material to input  
- **THEN:** speech to text can save a lot of time  

#### Prompt Iteration
- **IF:** the initial LLM response is not satisfactory  
- **THEN:** provide follow-up prompts to trigger Sierra to regenerate an improved result  

#### "First, ask me questions"
- **IF:** you are uncertain of what information Sierra needs to generate an accurate result  
- **THEN:** tell it to ask you clarifying questions prior to responding  

#### "Write a better prompt"
- **IF:** you’ve iteratively improved a request AND you plan to repeat the request in the future  
- **THEN:** ask Sierra to generate a better initial prompt for you to use next time

## What is fine-tuning?

### Core Ideas
- Fine-tuning is doing additional training on an already-trained LLM.
  - It usually diminishes the general accuracy of the LLM.
- It can improve an LLM's knowledge of specific information or styles.
  - It is a way to convert context into "internal knowledge."
- Think of it like brainwashing... the brainwashed LLM may learn to respond properly to certain questions, but becomes unstable in other areas.
- By providing better context, you can generally produce the safe effects as fine-tuning, without the negative side-effects.
